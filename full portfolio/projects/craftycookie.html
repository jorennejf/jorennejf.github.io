CraftyCookieShop Interactive Prototype

Mar 2020


Overview


Client

CraftyCookieShop


Goal

The goal of this project was to help a local business launch their website by creating a high-fidelity interactive prototype. The design was simple: create a step-by-step form so that customers can easily order custom cookies.


    My Roles

  • Research
  • User Interface Design
  • Interactive Prototyping

    Tools

  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • InVision

Story

The owner of Moochie Pops and Cakes wanted to open up a separate business focusing on cookies. Since cookies are easier to mass produce, she decided to expand her business by marketing this new cookie shop.

I worked with the owner to design a mock-up of the site, a step-by-step form to order custom cookies. She gave me free range in deciding the branding and creating the logo.


Design Process


    Research

  1. Since the client gave me full creative control, I researched other bakery designs and compiled them into a moodboard. It seemed like a lot of them were very brightly colored and replicated some sort of cookie shape.

    2. Design & Prototype

  2. For the business's branding, I decided to go with something brightly colored but still professional. For the logo, I created a colorful cookie icon and paired it with a white serif font.
    Final Logo Design

  3. When the client approached me for this project, she already created the wireframes, so I used these to design the high-fidelity prototype.
    Landing Page Wireframe
    Step-by-Step Form Wireframe

  4. Because the main goal was to get customers to send in a cookie order, I kept the design simple and limited the interactions on the website. The landing page only had call-to-actions that led to the order form and a hamburger menu in case users wanted to get extra information. The form itself was separated in 4 steps so that the user can focus on inputting the necessary information.

Conclusion

Unfortunately, even though the client was happy with my high-fidelity prototype, the website was never built. The COVID-19 pandemic prevented her from expanding her business to open up this stand-alone cookie shop. The client, however, continues to bake cookies as part of her other baking business.
